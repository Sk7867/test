Amul

Introduction

Amul derives its name from Sanskrit word “Amoolya” which means priceless. It is one of the most successful and major Indian brands is especially known for its milk products. Amul has been recognized by its famous jingle “utterly butterly delicious” and the charming mascot, “the Amul girl”. People have grown up watching and listening to the girl and the jingle on hoardings and advertisements. Amul, founded in 1946 has been the most trusted, beloved, and esteemed brand of India ever since and it has come a long way since its foundation.

History

Amul had its seeds sown 74 years ago at Anand district in the state of Gujarat. The farmers of Kaira were depended on Seasonal crops for their income, and during the off-season, they were deprived of food and faced starvation. Since the milk market was completely in the hands of contractors and middlemen, their income from milch buffaloes was unreliable. They were often asked to sell their milk at low or whatever price they were offered by contractors and then later sold at much higher prices by private companies. They sought help from Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel to stop the exploitation by private companies.

Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel asked them to eliminate middlemen and to undertake a strike. Due to such injustice and unfair practices, farmers called off for a “milk strike” for about 15 days. Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel had an insight to completely get rid of middlemen and to form a co-operative movement by the farmers. He wanted to have farmers complete control over the milk market and a co-operative that would have procurement, processing, and marketing. Hence, under the inspiration and guidance of Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel, Morarji Desai, and Tribhuvandas Patel a co-operative society was formed on 14th December 1946. This movement was called “the Kaira District Cooperative Milk Producer’s Union Limited” (KDCMPUL) which was successfully led by farmers to eradicate unfair trade practices by Polson and Polson Dairy. The movement grew and later in 1973 it formed into Gujarat Co-operative Milk Marketing Federation (GCMMF). The Union started producing 250 litres/day of milk with just a handful of farmers from two villages. Along with the success of the movement, more farmers started to join the milk societies. And with around 432 farmers, the production of milk increased to 5000 litres/day.

Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel wanted to lay the foundation of dairy equipment for the union and hence he asked Dr. Verghese Kurien to help him lay the dairy. Dr. Verghese Kurien from being a civil engineer was known to become the largest milk producer in the world. He was acknowledged as “Father of White Revolution” or “Dudhwala of India”. Kurien has been at the various positions throughout his career starting as Executive Head Of Kaira union in 1950 to founder chairman of the National Dairy Development Board from 1965 to 1998. He also led the “Operation Flood” the largest dairy development program in the world. He has also served GCMMF for more than 30 years and has been credited for success of Amul marketing. Dr. Verghese Kurien helped from the foundation of Kaira Union to leading the brand “Amul” today. With such a rare vision and dedication of Dr.Verghese Kurien, Amul has become the largest and most successful milk producing brand today.

Why So Successful?

Amul is the leading milk-producing brand and at top of its game, we often wonder what is the secret behind its success?. How did Amul manage to reach such heights?

Here are a few key points,

  • ● Farmers owned the dairy
  • ● Elected representatives managed the village societies
  • ● Employed professionals to operate dairy and manage its business
  • ● Operatives were sensitive to the needs and demands of their farmers

The company is particularly regulated and controlled by farmers. Every three years elections are held the most deserving candidate is elected as the chairperson of the company. The company makes sure that all the farmers actively participate so that it can provide democratic governance. The company takes under consideration that all the needs and requirements of the farmers are fulfilled. The company made sure that they establish the trust of the farmers and maintain the transparency in finance and governance.

  • ● The Amul Girl: The cute little girl, with a polka-dotted dress, is the true superstar, who has captured all the hearts and attention. She plays the perfect Indian girl who can get away with audacious comments. She came into existence in 1966 and since she has been capturing all hearts with her aura and cuteness. The recognition and capital surge that the brand has received is enormous. The interactive advertisements have not only drawn the attention of the consumers but also given global recognition. This little charm has truly played her magic.
  • ● Sustainability
  • ● Co-ordinated delivery Services

Strategies

Amul has quite effective and prominent strategies which look after targeting the mass population. Amul strategies cover various aspects right from pricing strategy to segmentation. Here are some of its marketing strategies

  • ● Pricing Strategy: Amul has mostly targeted and tried to capture the public belonging to the middle and lower class. Hence it has strategically planned its pricing keeping the Indian Market segments in mind. Amul offers lower prices on the daily essential items and at better quality than other competitors.

● Segmentation, Targeting and Positioning

Amul’s segmentation strategy is to target the mass population by offering various daily dairy products. We usually see that the Amul’s audience generally contains gender and age of people from different statistics. Amul has not owned to single product strategy which acts as a plus point for the company. It has produced a variety of products varying from ice-creams, cheese, ghee, butter, and other milk products. The company does not discriminate among its audience but uses mass population strategy. This strategy has therefore been quite effective and worked well for the company.

Amul has very well used the positioning strategy by engaging its audience and being a top brand when it comes to dairy products. It is always on top of mind whenever we think of milk products.

Brand Equity

With targeting the mass population, positioning and setting the corrects pricing standards, strong and reliable distribution, effective supply chain and the lucky mascot “the Amul girl”, Amul’s brand equity has been raised to an impressive spot and has a turnover of 45,000 crores and is said to achieve 50,000 crore target by 2020-2021.

Current Chairperson

Shri. Ramsinh Prabhatsinh Parmar was elected as the chairperson of the Kaira District Milk Marketing District in the Year 2019 from Thasra assembly seat. Mr. Parmar being the former Congress MLA has been re-elected for the past seven times and has been in the position since. He was also elected as a chairperson of GCMMF. He was also on the board of Amul dairy since 1978 and later he was elected as chairperson in 2002.
